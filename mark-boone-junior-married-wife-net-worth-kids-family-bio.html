Mark Boone Bio

The talented actor was born on 17 March 1955 in the United States. His birth name was Mark Heidrich and he was born in Cincinnati, Ohio, but grew up in Chicago with his parents Ginny and Bob Heidrich. He is of German, Scottish and English descent.

During his high school years, Mark was a football player, a game he played until his university days. After high school, he attended the University of Vermont, where he is a member of the men’s soccer team. It was at college that Mark began performing stand-up comedy, using his stage name “Boone”, which he has retained to this day. After moving to New York City, he performed with his friend Steve Buscemi. In his opinion, the name “Boone” was inspired by the War Memorial in New York City.

Career

After his college graduation, he moved to New York where he began his film career. In 1983 he played the role of a porn client/business manager in the movie “Variety” and in the same year he also played the lead in “Born in Flames”.

In 1989 Mark played the role of Hall in the television series “The Equalizer”. In the early ’90s, the talented actor played the role of Schockley in “Die Hard 2” and he also appeared in the reality TV series “Law & Order”, “Quantum Leap” and “Seinfeld”.

Boone, however, became a prominent character in movies, and in 1995 he played the role of a sleazy and corrupt policeman in the movie called “Seven” and in 2003 he played the role of detective Whitworth in 2 Fast 2 Furious. He came into the spotlight in 2005 after starring as Detective Flass in the popular movie “Batman Begins” and in the television series Sons of Anarchy from 2008 to 2014.

The icon has also played minor roles in films such as Armageddon and Oz, and he has also been recognized for his tangible contributions to the American film industry.

He was honored by the Dahlonega International Film Festival in 2003 as the best supporting actor for Greasewood. Over the course of his career, Mark Boone Junior has starred in over 70 films, and more are still to come.
