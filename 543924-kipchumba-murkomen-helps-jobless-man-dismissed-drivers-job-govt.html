Kipchumba Murkomen Secures Job for Man Who Reached out to Him at NTSA: &quot;Alikuwa na Mipango&quot;

 Posted on February 3, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  691 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill
  • CS Kipchumba Murkomen's handlers now say he was misunderstood when he told a jobseeker that he doesn't offer jobs at the NTSA offices
  • Murkomen was at the NTSA premises to inspect the transfer of the agency's services to the E-Citizen platform when he met with Elphus Musisa
  • The man seized the opportunity and told Murkomen that his main challenge was joblessness, but the CS would dismiss him saying there were no jobs offered there

Kai Eli, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

It now emerges that Roads and Transport CS Kipchumba Murkomen was working behind the scenes to help Elphus Musisa get employment in government.

Musisa became the talk of the town after his interaction with the CS who dismissed him after he put forth his job request.

Murkomen was at the National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) offices to inspect the transfer of the agency's services to the E-Citizen platform when he met with Musisa who had gone to check on his badge.

Despite Musisa's sorry state, Murkomen told him he doesn't offer jobs, irking Kenyan netizens who faulted him for being dismissive and insensitive to a needy case.

But the CS's handlers say he immediately directed them to find the man a suitable post after they met.

"Even before we left the NTSA premises the CS had asked that the man be assigned somewhere. When he said he doesn't offer jobs there, he meant at the premises," a handler revealed.

The Vihiga county native will now be a driver in a Kenya National Highway Authority (KeNHA) project in Nakuru.

The CS's inner team said he had been involved in many charitable acts and that he never purposed to broadcast them.

"Murkomen doesn’t do things for personal glory or to please the public. There are many people the CS helps, but we don’t go shouting about it. Unbeknownst to many people Murkomen has always been a compassionate many. He has helped many people," said his people.

They further explained that when the CS said he didn't give jobs, he meant at the NTSA offices, but not that he never helped people with jobs.

"What he said in the video was that 'we don't give jobs here at NTSA offices'. He didn’t say he doesn't offer jobs. In fact, before he left the NTSA building he had already instructed his people to get the man and help him secure a job," said one of the CS's point men.

Speaking after the offer, Musisa dismissed the claims he was maltreated by the CS, implying he was taken out of context.

According to him, despite the nasty show, Murkomen went and engaged his people whom he instructed to offer him (Musisa) a place in government.

They later traced him and asked him to report to work.

"Through Murkomen I have found a job in government. I want to thank him and President William Ruto. I had hunted for jobs since the late Daniel Moi's era and came to the late Mwai Kibaki's, and then Uhuru Kenyatta. I never got a job in all those regimes," said Musisa.

Why NTSA is integrating services into E-Citizen

Meanwhile, NTSA has been aligning the accounts of individual motorists and organisations with the E-Citizen platform since late last year.

The agency disabled the Transport Integrated Management System (TIMS) portal to allow the integration of services into E-Citizen.
