CAA Opens The Books For New Agents

 Posted on August 27, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  172 words  |  Larita Shotwell

LOS ANGELES (CelebrityAccess) — CAA has promoted ten trainees at the company to serve as agents or an executive role.

According to the Hollywood Reporter, the lion's share of the promotions were in the music department. Janet Kim, Phil Quist and Lanell Rumion are all newly minted music agents in the company's Los Angeles office. David Ball is working out of the London office and Ben Schildkraut will be based in New York.

Matt Brownstein was named as the company's newest baseball agent, and Allison Wruble has been appointed as an executive in the company's Innovation group.

Angela Dallas and Ali Spiesman have both been named as new television agents, and will be based out of the company's Los Angeles and New York offices respectively.

London-based Natalie Drye has been named to an executive post in CAA's Sports property sales department, which has sold more than $3.5 billion worth of new sponsorship business, including deals with Yankees Stadium, Madison Square Garden and other venues, the Hollywood Reporter said. – Staff Writers
