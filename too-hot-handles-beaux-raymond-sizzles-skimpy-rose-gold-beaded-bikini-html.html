She was no stranger to stripping down to her swimwear on Too Hot to Handle.

And series three winner Beaux Raymond, 24, looked sensational as she wore a beaded thong bikini in snaps released on Wednesday.

It comes as it emerged the reality star has been 'inundated with offers' and is set to make big bucks after signing to prestigious talent agency Neon Management alongside the likes of former Love Island star Liberty Poole and one-time TOWIE regular Joey Essex.

Beaux wore lashings of make-up to highlight her pretty facial features and her long blonde hair tumbled down onto her shoulders and back.

The television personality won series three of Netflix show Too Hot to Handle, splitting the $90,000 (£66,850) with fellow contestant Harry Johnson.

Just 24 hours after making her debut on the show with a bevy of other singletons in the Turks and Caicos Islands, Beaux and the other contestants were told they were not allowed to touch each other as there was no sexual contact.

Any intimate moments between them would see the total prize fund of $200,000 (£148,570) decrease.

Despite initially setting their sights on other people, Beaux and Harry fell for each other.

The pair kept their eyes on the prize and stuck to the rules for the majority of the show, apart from Harry losing $4,000 (£2,970) after choosing to spend some time alone.

Beaux's success on the show has seen her sign to Neon Management, with the influencer expected to rake in the cash this year.

A representative for the star told MailOnline: 'We have been inundated with offers for Beaux and are very excited to start working with her on so many exciting projects.' 

Taking to Instagram this week, she gave fans an update on her relationship status with Harry after the finale of Too Hot to Handle.

Posting a picture of herself getting very close to Harry as the pair enjoyed a sweet treat, she wrote: 'You are what you eat.. so make it something sweet.'

On Wednesday, Harry gave fans a glimpse at their romantic London date as they posed for a photo by the River Thames in front of Tower Bridge.

He captioned his post: 'Scenic... Company isn’t bad either.'
