Neighbours Jane Harris star's life - real name, snubbed roles and meeting husband on set

 Posted on May 28, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  880 words  |  Aldo Pusey

After TV appearances in Henderson Kids and Sons and Daughters, Annie Jones soon became a household after landing the role of Neighbours' Jane Harris as a 19-year old in 1986.

The actress originally auditioned for the role of Charlene Mitchell, missing out to a certain Kylie Minogue, who she would soon go on to star alongside in Erinsborough. Known as 'Plain Jane Superbrain' for her geeky persona, Annie retired the character after just three years to focus on other projects including leading roles in Jackaroo and Newlyweds.

She stepped away from the limelight completely, however, in 1994 in order to care for her mum for almost two decades in her battle with Alzheimer's Disease. After a couple of cameos in 2018 and a further appearance to mark Neighbours' 35th anniversary in 2020, Annie made a permanent return to the Ramsay Street cast later that year.

Indeed, Annie was back in the thick of the action immediately alongside fellow veterans of the show, Harold Bishop (played by Ian Smith) and Dr. Carl Kennedy (Alan Fletcher) when it returned to screens on September 18. Bosses have promised the star some major storylines - not least this week which sees dramatic scenes unfold over in England where Jane is visiting old haunts with the love of her life, Mike Young (Guy Pearce).

Now, join us as delve further into the life and times of Annie's career.

Real name and early days

Annie was actually born Annika Jansco in Adelaide to Hungarian immigrant parents in 1967. After a modelling stint in her teens, Annie decided to take on her Anglicised stage name as she launched her career as an actress.

It was her breakthrough role as Sylvia Wheeler in the aforementioned The Henderson Kids where she would meet her husband and director of the show, Paul Moloney. The pair married in 1989 and are still together to this day.

Snubbed roles

In a 2020 interview Annie revealed she had spent two decades turning down job offers as she devoted her time to caring for her mum, who sadly passed away in 2016. At the time, she explained to the Mirror: "I don't regret it [pausing her career]. It’s probably made me a better person in many ways. I think you learn from the stuff that life throws at you, that’s what it’s all about, life experiences.

"It’s made me a different actress too, empathy is the number one most important thing for any actor. Anyone who has had anything to do with Alzheimer's or dementia will know what it’s like. It’s heartbreaking, seeing someone disappear before your eyes."

Annie did make a handful of guest appearances during that period however, with a notable three-episode role in Aussie prison drama Wentworth in 2013 and a cameo in The Secret Life of Us in 2002.

Double reprise

Given a new lease of life in 2020, Annie saw Charlotte Chimes cast as Jane's estranged daughter, Nicolette Stone. Discussing her reprising of the role, she told Radio Times: "Jane is returning to her old neighbourhood and bringing with her an insight into what her life away from Ramsay Street has been like for the past 30 years. Audiences will learn more about her family, as fans are introduced to her daughter Nicolette for the first time."

"It's like being in a little timewarp for me," she continued. "I'm back in the 80s, I'm 19 years old, going to work at Nunawading studios in Melbourne every day - and loving it. I'm really thankful to be back."

As the show wound down two years later, seemingly for good, Jane shared emotional scenes with Mike, reuniting after almost 30 years to offer him a tour of Ramsay Street with its properties all listed for sale. Following flashbacks to iconic 80s scenes that saw Jane ditch her nerdy glasses to become a blonde bombshell, Mike reminded her that she was beautiful with or without her specs.

The end? No! Reboot scenes airing later this week will see Jane faced with a big decision concerning her relationship with Mike over in Surrey, before her return to Ramsay Street from England is blighted by one of her close friendships being called into question.
