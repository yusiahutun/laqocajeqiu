Samuel Mugoh Muvota: DCI Says Slain Man Had Fallen out with Partners

 Posted on October 3, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  523 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • The Directorate of Criminal Investigation said Samuel Mugo was a thug who allegedly started conning Kenyans in 2011
  • According to the DCI, Mugo had fallen out with his friends before meeting his death on the afternoon of May 16 in Mirema
  • It is still not clear who might have killed the Mugo as the DCI are going on with investigations into the matter

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has said that the man who was shot dead in broad daylight had fallen out with his partners.

The 40-year-old Samuel Mugoh Muvota was killed by an unknown assailant on the afternoon of Monday, May 16, in a well-orchestrated execution that was captured by nearby CCTV cameras.

In a lengthy narration, the investigative body said that Mugo was not on good terms with friends who fled the country after learning about his death.

"Preliminary investigations indicate that he had fallen out with some of his partners, who fled to a neighbouring country immediately after the murder. Detectives are hot on their trail," the DCI said.

The DCI suspect that the slain man might have been carrying out his illicit activities with his friends, who are on the run.

Mugo conning people

The investigative organ also revealed that Mugoh was a serial conman who had terrorised many people in the country.

The father of three allegedly began conning unsuspecting businessmen and club revellers in 2011.

He began his trade by hanging around city ATMs and offering to assist customers who had difficulties withdrawing money from their accounts.

He worked with crooked banking officials who would jam the teller machines and once a client inserted the ATM card, Mugo would approach the client offering to assist and would then go on to rob them.

At one time in 2018, he transferred KSh 700,000 in a single transaction from an account of a woman he was assisting through Pesalink to one of his fraudulent accounts, leaving a balance of only KSh 700.

When the woman learnt that her money had been stolen, she collapsed and died on the spot.

"The woman had walked to the ATM booth to check whether a loan she had applied at the bank had been deposited in her account when she met the Muvota. When she learnt of what had happened, she collapsed and died," the DCI said.

Arrested over 30 times

Being a criminal, Mugo had run-ins with the law in the course of his decade-long criminal life and had even been arrested 30 times.

On most occasions, he would approach the complainants with a refund for their money and the cases would collapse without him facing any case or jail time.
