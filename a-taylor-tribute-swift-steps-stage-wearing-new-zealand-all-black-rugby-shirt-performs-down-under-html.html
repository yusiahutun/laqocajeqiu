She may be known for her love of girly, floaty fashion, but Taylor Swift made a more sporty addition to her stage wear as she performed in New Zealand earlier tonight.  

The 22-year-old singer paid tribute to the country's All Blacks rugby team for a concert in Auckland.

And the star looked pleased as punch with the reaction her outfit received from the audience, pointing out the feather emblem to her fans.

All black tribute: Taylor Swift sported a New Zealand rugby shirt as she performed a concert in Auckland New Zealand earlier today

But along with her tribute, the star still sported her more familiar ultra feminine full flouncy and sequinned skirt.

Bright red lipstick and wavy blonde hair completed the look as she performed at the city's Vector Arena.

However, her tribute didn't last the entire night - she later changed into an pretty white floaty dress and played the banjo during one number.

Girly touch: The singer teamed her black rugby shirt with a flouncy beige sequinned skirt

Flying the feather: Taylor showed the crowd her solidarity with the New Zealand emblem at the beginning of her performance

For another song, she sported a gold fringed outfit and whipped her hair as she rocked out to a guitar based number.

The star is touring Australia and New Zealand as part of her Speak Now tour.

Along with her black rugby shirt, she endeared herself to the crowd by telling them she thought their country was 'the most beautiful place on earth.'

The white stuff: Taylor then returned to her more girly stage look of a floaty white dress

Unlucky for some: Taylor had the numbers 13 written on the back of her hand - which was the title of her first album

She added: 'This is the first time I’ve ever stood on a stage in New Zealand and I’ve got to say Auckland, this is quite a first impression.'

And she had a special treat in store for her fans as she used the concert to perform her newest song, Eyes Open, which is from the Hunger Games soundtrack, live for the first time.

She teased the audience as she said, 'You don’t think I’d get in trouble if I played it now? Probably not, right? I don’t know, what do you think?' before bursting into song.

The star had her usual string of handwritten lyrics along her forearm and her lucky number '13' written in bold ink on the back of her hand.

'I paint this on my hand before every show because 13 is my lucky number - for a lot of reasons,' she explained to MTV once. 'It's really weird.'

Whip my hair: Taylor changed into a gold fringed dress as she rocked out with a red glittery guitar

The final curtain: Wearing a peach coloured ball gown and surrounded by confetti, Taylor took her final bow with her band

Going green: Taylor posted this photo to her Twitter page along with the caption: 'Celebrating St Patrick's Day the best way I know how. Donuts'

'I was born on the 13th. I turned 13 on Friday the 13th. My first album went gold in 13 weeks. My first number one song had a 13-second intro,' she said.

'Every time I've won an award I've been seated in either the 13th seat, the 13th row, the 13th section or row M, which is the 13th letter.

'Basically whenever a 13 comes up in my life, it's a good thing.'

Pretty in polka dots: Taylor was seen earlier this week doing some sight-seeing in New Zealand

Meanwhile, Taylor was spotted out earlier this week indulging in some sight-seeing in the New Zealand city.

And she looked pretty in a orange spotted dress and mustard coloured cardigan as she strolled around with friends.

She wore her trademark red lipstick and a matching red hair  band along with bright blue ballet pumps.
