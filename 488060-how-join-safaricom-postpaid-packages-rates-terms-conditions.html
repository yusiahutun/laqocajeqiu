How to join Safaricom postpaid: packages, rates, terms and conditions

 Posted on August 24, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1154 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The Safaricom PostPay service offers customers the freedom to call, text, and browse the web at affordable rates. The advantage of using the service is that you pay at the end of the month. Furthermore, Safaricom PostPay has packages that cater for individuals with different budgets. You can subscribe to a service for as low as Ksh 1,000 or as high as Ksh 10,000 per month. But how do you join Safaricom postpaid service?

You can join Safaricom postpaid in three ways. The first method is through USSD, the second is through mySafaricom app, and the third is via the web. However, joining via USSD and mySafaricom app are the most straightforward methods because they take a few minutes.

Generally, the process requires you to choose and pay for a package. Safaricom will then migrate your line to PostPay and start enjoying a 30-day convenience service.

Ways to replace Safaricom line without original PIN

The easiest way to join Safaricom PostPay is through USSD. It will allow you to see your predetermined credit limit and pay for the service. Here is what to do:

  • Dial *544# on your phone.
  • Reply with option 4 labelled GO MONTHLY.
  • Reply with option 4 labelled Join PostPay.
  • At the Choose Journey section, reply with option 2 labelled Continue with USSD.
  • A list of your predetermined credit limit will appear.
  • Choose any option you prefer. In this case, consider the Ksh 1,000 package.
  • A popup will appear informing you to activate your PostPay Tariff. Since your USSD code is running, you must ensure your Safaricom line has a balance of KES 1,000 (credit limit). Otherwise, top up your line to continue or decline the activation.
  • Enter your email address.
  • Accept the Safaricom PostPay terms and conditions.

At this point, you can join PostPay and continue using the service. If you have mySafaricom app on your Android or iOS device, you can use it to join Safaricom PostPay.

  • Open the mySafaricom app.
  • Select the PostPay icon.
  • You will see a predetermined credit limit.
  • If you do not see a credit limit, enter your preferred limit.
  • Select a PostPay bundle plan.
  • Enter your email address.
  • Accept the terms and conditions.
  • Proceed to pay via M-PESA.

You will receive a notification confirming your Tariff has migrated to PostPay.

What are the PostPay packages from Safaricom?

You can choose from five Safaricom PostPay packages. The five packages are as follows:

  • Ksh 1,000 – You will get 400 Safaricom PostPay minutes, 5GB data, 1,000 SMS, free 2GB YouTube, and WhatsApp.
  • Ksh 2,000 – You will get 1,000 minutes, 15GB of data, 2,000 SMS, and free 2GB of YouTube and WhatsApp.
  • Ksh 3,000 – You will get 1,500 minutes, 25GB of data, 3,000 SMS, and free 2GB of YouTube and WhatsApp.
  • Ksh 5,000 – You will get 2,500 minutes, 45GB data, 5,000 SMS, free 2GB YouTube, and WhatsApp.
  • Ksh 10,000 – You will get 7,500 minutes, 100GB of data, 10,000 SMS, and free 2GB of YouTube and WhatsApp.

The bundles you receive have no expiry if they remain after 30 days, which is their validity period. Also, you can use the minutes to call internationally, including China, India, Canada, and the United States.

You cannot use the bundle for roaming. However, they will auto-renew every 1st of the month. After subscribing to the PostPay service, you can contact the Safaricom PostPaid customer care service on 200.

What happens when the bundle exhausts before the end of the month?

Generally, the Safaricom PostPay packages do not expire. Furthermore, you can only purchase them once a month. So, when your bundle exhausts before the auto-renewal, Safaricom will charge you Ksh 2.30 for calls and Ksh 1.30 for data.

The amounts will apply once you run out of the bundle. However, you can purchase a bundle with expiry or non-expiry dates using *544#.

Safaricom uses the email you provided to send you a bill. You will receive the bill on the third of every month. Once you get it in your email, you must clear it by the 16th of the month.

If you fail to settle your bill after the stipulated date, Safaricom will disconnect you from PostPay. As a result, you will have to return to Pre-Pay.

Who is eligible for the PostPay service?

All Safaricom customers who use the prepay service and are on the Uwezo Tariff can subscribe to the PostPay service. However, the service is not open to corporate accounts.

How to pay for Safaricom PostPay service

You can pay for PostPay service via M-PESA, Bonga Points, or credit limit. In both cases, you require the Safaricom PostPay Paybill number. Here is how:

  • Open the M-PESA menu on your phone.
  • Select PayBill.
  • Enter the PayBill number 200200.
  • Enter the mobile number to purchase the PostPay service.
  • Enter the amount corresponding to your package.
  • Enter your M-PESA PIN.

You can now start using your Tariff to make calls, send texts, and browse the web. If you have many Bonga Points, you can use them to pay for Safaricom’s PostPay service. Here is how:

  • Dial *126# from your phone.
  • Reply with option 2 labelled Lipa Na Bonga Points.
  • Select Pay Bill.
  • Enter the Business Number or PayBill number 200200.
  • Enter the amount corresponding to your PostPay Tariff.
  • Enter your Service PIN.
  • Confirm your transaction.

You will receive a confirmation message with a transaction ID and another message indicating you have subscribed to PostPay.

You can use *544# to purchase your preferred package if your line has enough funds. Safaricom will use your credit limit to activate the service on your line.

How to check PostPay balance

You can check your PostPay balance by dialling *544#. The option will give you the balance of all the resources remaining, including data and minutes. Also, you can use the same USSD code to stop the auto-renewal of your bundle.
