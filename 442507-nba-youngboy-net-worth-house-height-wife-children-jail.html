Early life

The singer was born Kentrell DeSean Gaulden, on October 20, 1999, in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, U.S. However, he is famously known by his stage names, YoungBoy Never Broke Again, NBA YoungBoy, or simply YoungBoy.

He has three siblings Ken Gaulden, Telee Gaulden, and BWay Yungy Ka. He dropped out of school in the ninth grade. He was arrested for robbery and sent to a detention centre in Tallulah, Louisiana. While there, he began writing lyrics for his debut project.

Upon his release, his grandmother had passed away, and as a result, he moved in with his friend and fellow rapper NBA 3Three. Unfortunately, the duo committed various crimes to pay for time in the studio.

Career

At the age of 14, YoungBoy bought a mic from Walmart and began producing music with it and this marked the beginning of his career. In 2015, he released his first mixtape, Life Before Fame. Later, he released several others such as Mind of a Menace, Mind of a Menace 2, and Before I Go.

Gaulden made a hit with his October 2016 mixtape, 38 Baby, featuring fellow Baton Rouge natives, Boosie Badazz, Kevin Gates, and fellow rappers Stroke Tha Don and NBA 3Three. One week later, he released another mixtape titled Mind of a Menace.

In November 2016, the rapper was arrested in Austin, Texas, for suspicion of attempted first-degree murder connected to a drive-by shooting. He was jailed in East Baton Rouge Parish, Louisiana. While there, he re-released his two mixtapes, Before I Go and Mind of a Menace 3.

NBA YoungBoy was released from prison in May 2017 after taking a plea deal and posting bail. A week after leaving prison, Gaulden released the single, Untouchable. In July 2017, the star released a video for his song, 41, including cameos from notable artists, including Meek Mill, Young Thug, 21 Savage, Boosie Badazz, and Yo Gotti.

NBA YoungBoy's legal issues

Despite his success, NBA YoungBoy's career has been faced with a long history of legal issues that began in 2016. In several cases, he has released projects during his incarcerations. The legal issues include;

  • On November 28, 2016, he was charged with two counts of attempted murder.
  • On February 25, 2018, he was arrested for allegedly committing assault, weapons violations, and kidnapping.
  • On May 12, 2019, he was involved in a shooting in Miami.
  • On September 28, 2020, he was arrested in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, on charges of distribution and manufacturing of drugs and possession of stolen firearms.
  • On March 22, 2021, Gaulden was arrested by federal agents executing a federal warrant.
