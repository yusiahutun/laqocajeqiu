Meaning of What The Price by Migos

 Posted on July 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  435 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

The overall theme of the Migos song titled "What The Price" centers around the idea of the cost of success and the sacrifices one must make to achieve their goals. The song explores the various ways in which individuals navigate the pursuit of their desires and the often high price they have to pay to attain them.One standout line in the song is "Tell me what the preacher preach about, tell me what the teacher teach about, I'ma go find me a better route." This lyric delves into the notion of challenging conventional wisdom and seeking alternative paths to success. It suggests that the traditional avenues taught by preachers and teachers may not always align with the individual's aspirations. The theme here is the importance of carving out one's own path and not simply adhering to societal or educational norms.Another notable lyric is "Pay what you owe, eighty a show." This line can be interpreted as a reflection of the costs associated with success in the music industry. It highlights the financial obligations and demands placed on artists, underscoring the sacrifices they make to pursue their passion. The theme here revolves around the price of fame and the monetary sacrifices necessary to maintain a thriving career as an artist.The lyric "Got racks in the back of my pants" brings attention to the idea of material wealth and the desire for financial abundance. It speaks to the pursuit of material possessions and luxurious lifestyles that often accompany success. This theme explores the concept of materialism and the sacrifices individuals are willing to make to attain wealth and the associated social status.Another memorable line in the song is "Bad Mona Lisa, slide with my people, pink slip for the ride, but what's in the trunk, it's illegal." This lyric touches on the theme of living on the edge and engaging in activities that may be seen as against the law or morally questionable. It implies a rebellious nature and a disregard for societal norms in the pursuit of personal fulfillment and excitement. The theme here speaks to the willingness to take risks and push boundaries in order to achieve one's desires.In conclusion, the Migos song "What The Price" explores the theme of the cost of success and the sacrifices individuals must make to attain their dreams. Through various standout lyrics, the song delves into concepts such as challenging conventional wisdom, financial obligations, materialism, and living on the edge. Ultimately, these themes highlight the lengths individuals are willing to go to in order to achieve their goals and the diverse paths they may take to get there.
