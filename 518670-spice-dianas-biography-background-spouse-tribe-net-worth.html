Who is Spice Diana?

Spice Diana is a Ugandan musician who became famous with her first single, Onsanula. She has been active in the music industry for nearly a decade, mostly singing Rhythm and Blues in the Buganda dialect.

Spice was born in 1996 and will be 27 years old in 2023. She was born in Mpererwe, Kampala district and was raised by Nantale Beatrice as a single mother. Not much is known about Spice Diana's father.

According to Spice’s mother, she was forced to get married at 14 and became pregnant after a few months, giving birth to Spice. Her mother later moved to Kibuli and married another person with whom they also separated.

Spice Diana attended Kibuli Demonstration School for her primary education and St Peter’s Senior Secondary School for her secondary education. Later, Spice went to Makerere University, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Industrial Arts.

While in school, the talented musician struggled to balance music and her studies as most of her lecture times collided with her musical performances.

Spice’s career

Spice didn’t ever imagine that she would be in the music industry. Her music career got the breakthrough she sought in 2014 after producing her first hit song, Onsanula. At this time, she was managed by producer Fizol of Avie Records. Spice was linked to the producer by Geosteady.

In 2015, the renowned musician collaborated with Pallaso to release another hit song, Koona, under Avie Records. Spice then left Avie Records for Twinkle Star Management of Emma Carlos, who was then the manager of Khalifa Aganga.
