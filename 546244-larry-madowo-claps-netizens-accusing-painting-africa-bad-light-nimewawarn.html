Larry Madowo Claps Back at Netizens Accusing Him of Painting Africa in Bad Light: &quot;Nimewawarn&quot;

 Posted on September 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  485 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • Larry Madowo found himself on the receiving end after covering the Mathare flood in Nairobi county
  • He reminded people that his job was to cover Africa accurately and not positively after being accused of painting the continent negatively
  • Some netizens supported him and encouraged him to keep pushing the stories, while others claimed he was biased

CNN International journalist Larry Madowo has shaded people who came at him for reporting about floods in Nairobi and accused him of always focusing on negative stories in Africa.

Larry Madowo on reporting African stories

Madowo reminded them of a post he made in June 2023, where he said his job is to cover accurately and not just positively.

In the post, he shared his CNN profile and a quote many called him out for. His quote read:

"We are more than just diseases, drought and war. We have accomplishments, art, beauty, joy and innovation. My reporting should reflect that."

However, this is not the first time Madowo has found himself a loggerhead with people on social media.

He recently clashed with the Roads and Transport Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen after highlighting the plight of Jomo Kenyatta International Airport.

Madowo also reminded Africans that he had warned them about his reporting and vowed to continue reporting what was happening in the region.

Madowo walks through flooded Mathare slum

TUKO.co.ke recently featured the CNN’s international correspondent after encountering the devastating impact of the floods firsthand in Mabatini, Mathare.

In a video, he was seen wading through ankle-deep water on a submerged basketball court in Mathare, expressing solidarity with the affected community.

Madowo also expressed how shocked he was to see the effects of the flood and faulted responsible agencies for not offering any help.
