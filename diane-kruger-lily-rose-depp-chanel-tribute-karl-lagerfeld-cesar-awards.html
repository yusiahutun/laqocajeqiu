Diane Kruger and Lily-Rose Depp pay sartorial tribute to Karl Lagerfeld at the 2019 Csar Awards

 Posted on May 16, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  325 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Diane Kruger and Lily-Rose Depp paid sartorial homage to Karl Lagerfeld, who passed away on Wednesday aged 85, in Chanel gowns at the 2019 César Awards.

Last night, Kruger graced the star-studded red carpet in a gold feathered mini dress with a sheer full-length underlay.

Getty Images

The actress dressed in a chic mini dress by Chanel for the awards ceremony

The actress finished the ensemble with minimal make-up and jewellery by the French fashion house.

Joining her in the tribute, model and Chanel muse, Lily-Rose Depp, wore a couture lace midi dress with satin courts also by the designer.

Getty Images

French model-turned-actress Lily-Rose Depp, 19, also wore a Chanel number to the starry ceremony

At the A-list event - often dubbed the 'French Oscars' - Kruger also made a speech in honour of Lagerfeld.

"A few days ago, a light went out. He was my friend. A friend of cinema and a friend to actresses," she said. "I miss him already, I miss him terribly. I am just happy to have said a last goodbye."

Getty Images

Diane Kruger took to the stage to give a speech in honour of the late designer

The 42-year-old actress continued, "It's impossible to imagine climbing the steps in Cannes or an Oscar ceremony without one of his mythical creations. His sense of humour and worldview, often hilarious, will be missed."

In 2013, Kruger became the face of Chanel and worked alongside the designer for a number of year across several campaigns.

Shortly after news of his death broke, the new mum took to Instagram to reveal that she had hoped to introduce the designer to her daughter.

Lagerfeld was reportedly working hard close up until his death and even gave his team instructions relating to the Fendi autumn/winter 2019 show which took place on Thursday.

The late designer's right-hand woman and Chanel studio director, Virginie Viard, will succeed Lagerfeld as creative designer.
