It is normal for people to face issues in their lives that may force them to go under, until they come out of the situation. Such also holds true for celebrities like Mickey Rourke who has had his fair share of ups and downs. He disappeared from the public eye and then came back with notable changes, and fans have been left wondering what happened to Mickey Rourke.

Who is Mickey Rouke? Philip Andre "Mickey" Rourke Jr. is an American actor, music supervisor, former boxer, and screenwriter. He has appeared in several movies since 1979, for example, Iron Man 2 and The Expendables in 2010 and the Immortals in 2011. He started boxing as a teenager, but became a professional much later in 1991, although it has not been continuous.

What happened to Mickey Rourke?

Although Mickey was committed to boxing as a teenager, he fell in love with acting after participating in the play, Deathwatch. He then relocated to New York where he attended the famous Actors Studio.

The talented actor has had trouble maintaining success in Hollywood. In the 1980s, Mickey struggled with his flourishing fame. He was involved in scandalous relationships, misused his money, and tainted his name on set for being difficult to work with. He could not keep his attitude in check while on set and has been referred to as a "loose cannon" and a nightmare to work with.

Due to his bad behaviour that made him fall out of favour in Hollywood, Mickey got into a very dark time in his life. He started drinking excessively and his mental health was affected, leading to depression. At one point, he stayed indoors for almost five months and slept in his closet. With his career over, his wife gone, and no money, he only had his dogs. If you check Mickey Rourke Instagram page, you will clearly see that he is a dog lover, and this is because of the loyalty of his pets during his hardest times.

What happened to Mickey Rourke's face?

After his issues with acting, Mickey decided to switch to boxing in 1991. He said that he made the switch because he felt that he was self-destructing and had no respect for himself as an actor. Mickey Rourke's boxing career was good, with eight matches undefeated (six wins and two draws). He was an international fighter who fought in countries like Japan, Germany, and Spain.

As is always the case with boxing, injuries are prone. Mickey suffered several injuries such as a broken nose, split tongue, compressed cheekbone, and broke toes and ribs. Short-term loss of memory was also another side effect. Eventually, the injuries were too much and necessitated reconstructive surgery on his face.

Mickey Rouke wife

Mickey has been married twice in the past although he has no children. He was married to Debra Feuer in 1981 whom she met while working on the film Hardcase. The marriage lasted until 1989 although they remained good friends. From 1992 through 1998, Mr. Rouke was married to Carré Otis.
