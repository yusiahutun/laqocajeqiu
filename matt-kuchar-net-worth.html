Matt Kuchar’s Amatuer Career

Before turning professional, Matt Kuchar had some impressive aspects as an amateur golfer. He graduated in 1996 from Seminole High School and got admission in Georgia Tech in Atlanta, where he played various collegiate golf events and earned the title All-American twice.

He secured his victory at the Terra Cotta Invitational (1997) in the U.S. Amateur. In 1998, Matt became the nation's top collegiate golfer and also received the Haskins Award. During his amateur career Matt Kucher also competed at Walkers Cup (1998) and World Amateur Team Championship in 1998, representing the United States of America.

Matt Kuchar’s Professional Career

After graduating from Georgia Tech, Matt Kuchar became a professional golfer in the year 2000 and started playing on the PGA Tour. He won at the Honda Classic (2002), which was his first victory on the PGA Tour. During his professional career, Matt won a total of 18 professional tournaments including 9 PGA Tour. Besides the PGA Tour, he has also played on the European Tour, Japan Golf Tour, Asian Tour, PGA Tour of Australia, and the Korn Ferry Tour.

Throughout his professional career, Matt Kuchar has competed in each four major championships and made his best finishes, including T2 at The Open Championship in 2017. He reached his peak on June 2, 2013 with the 4th position in the Official World Golf Ranking. Additionally, he earned several awards due to his outstanding performance on the course, which includes the PGA Tour leading money winner in 2010, Vardon Trophy in 2010 and Byron Nelson Award in 2010.
