Aryna Sabalenka shares emotional embrace after winning match days after ex-boyfriend's 'suicide'

 Posted on March 9, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  752 words  |  Martina Birk

"Best friends" Aryna Sabalenka and Paula Badosa shared an emotional moment following their clash at the Miami Open.

Second seed Sabalenka dispatched a close companion in her first match since it emerged ex-boyfriend Konstantin Koltsov had died in Miami on Monday, aged 42. Miami-Dade Police ruled it an apparent suicide, and Sabalenka later confirmed she and the former NHL player had split prior to his death.

Tennis superstar Sabalenka, 25, made fairly short work of Badosa in her comeback fixture, winning 6-4, 6-3 to advance to the third round. And despite her exit from the tournament, the Spaniard appeared content it was such a firm friend that did the honours.

She added: "It wasn't very comfortable to play my best friend in this situation. . .honestly both of us are pretty strong mentally, we're strong women, I proved it, she proved it."

Sabalenka has remained largely out of the public eye this week but described her ex-partner's death as an "unthinkable tragedy." She added her "heart was broken" by the news despite no longer be in a relationship with the late hockey star, who once played for the Pittsburgh Penguins before moving into coaching.
