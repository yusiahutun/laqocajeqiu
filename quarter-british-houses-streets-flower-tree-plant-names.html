A Quarter Of British Houses Named After Flowers Most Popular Flower And Tree Street Names

 Posted on April 11, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  424 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

It’s that time of year when Chelsea Flower Show inspires lovers of all things gardens, plants and flowers to come out in their droves.

But it seems Brits don’t need a flower show to exhibit their appreciation of nature and gardens, as new research has revealed that a quarter of British houses (over 70,000) have monikers inspired by flowers, trees and foliage.

A Royal Mail survey has discovered there are over 100,000 British streets and houses with names inspired by plants and trees.

Royal Mail​​

Unsurprisingly, rural and farming areas tend to have more nature-inspired address names, with the picturesque location of Tonbridge being the flower moniker hotspot. This Kent town has over 2,300 addresses, while rural Norfolk, Ipswich and Peterborough closely follow. York, Gloucester, Guildford, Exeter, Portsmouth and Fife also seem to love a good flower or tree address name.

The national flowers of England and Scotland have proved to be some of the most popular choices – the rose and heather. Jasmine, lavender and primrose are also highly favoured.

Royal Mail​​

In terms of trees, the most popular address name is beech, with 8,298 entries, followed by hawthorn, yew, oak and elm.

Overall, there are more than 54,000 flower-related and almost 49,500 tree-related addresses in the UK. See the lists below for the most popular names…
