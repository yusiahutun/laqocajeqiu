Surgeon believes Delta Goodrem has had nose job, lip and cheek fillers

 Posted on September 27, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  481 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Delta Goodrem is known across the world for her striking appearance.

But a leading plastic surgeon believes the Born To Try singer, 33, may have invested in some cosmetic tweaks over the years.

Dr. Randal Haworth told Daily Mail Australia on Monday he has a 'strong hunch' that Delta may have undergone a rhinoplasty, and may have also had filler injected into her cheeks and lips.

'I have a strong hunch she has undergone a rhinoplasty': Beverly Hills cosmetic surgeon Dr. Randal Haworth believes that Delta Goodrem may have had a nose job as well as lip and cheek fillers to enhance her stunning looks. Pictured left in 2003 and right in 2017

'Discerning what plastic surgery a person had can be challenging even for seasoned professionals or relatively easy given the type of work they had. Delta, I feel, falls in the latter category,' said the CEO of DrHaworth.com.

The Beverly Hills surgeon believes the tip of Delta's nose is likely evidence that she has had cosmetic surgery.

He claimed: 'Certain angles maybe more revealing than others, but I have a strong hunch she had undergone a rhinoplasty or nose job in the past because the shape of her tip is more demarcated from the rest of her nose while her bridge is noticeably narrowed.'

'The shape of her tip is more demarcated from the rest of her nose while her bridge is noticeably narrowed': Dr. Haworth believes the tip of Delta's nose is likely evidence that she has had cosmetic surgery. Pictured left in 2003 and right in 2017

'These are the types of characteristics that would make any plastic surgeon suspicious,' he added.

Meanwhile, Dr. Haworth also believes the songstress may have indulged in some other non-surgical cosmetic procedures.

He says Delta's full cheeks and plump pout could be the result of temporary filler. 

'I find it apparent that both her cheekbones and her lips have been enhanced with a temporary filler': Dr. Haworth also believes that Delta may have indulged in some other non-surgical cosmetic procedures. Pictured left in 2005 and right in 2018

'I find it apparent that both her cheekbones and her lips have been enhanced with a temporary filler, most likely of a hyaluronic acid variety such as Juvéderm or Restylane' he told Daily Mail Australia.   

'Celebrities with busy schedules oftentimes can't afford the recovery time needed to undergo fat transfer for more permanent results,' he explained.

Daily Mail Australia has contacted Delta's management for comment.
