Hovsgol Lake is one of the oldest and deepest lakes in the world.

With an estimated age of over 2 million years, Hovsgol Lake is considered one of the oldest lakes on Earth. It is also one of the deepest freshwater lakes, with a maximum depth of 262 meters (860 feet)!

The lake is home to over 60 species of fish.

Hovsgol Lake boasts incredible aquatic biodiversity, housing over 60 species of fish, including the endemic Hovsgol grayling. This makes it a paradise for fishing enthusiasts and researchers alike.

It is surrounded by the magnificent Hovsgol Mountains.

The lake is nestled within the picturesque Hovsgol Mountains, which are part of the Sayan mountain range. The breathtaking scenery of snow-capped peaks, lush green valleys, and crystal-clear waters make it a haven for nature lovers.

Hovsgol Lake is a sacred site for the local Tsaatan people.

The Tsaatan people, who are a reindeer herding community, consider Hovsgol Lake to be a sacred and spiritual place. They believe that it is inhabited by ancestral spirits and guardians.

The lake freezes over completely during winter.

Due to its high latitude and harsh climate, Hovsgol Lake freezes over completely during the winter months, creating a stunning icy landscape. This frozen wonderland attracts visitors from around the world.

Hovsgol National Park surrounds the lake.

The lake is situated within Hovsgol National Park, a protected area spanning over 8,638 square kilometers (3,337 square miles). The park is home to diverse flora and fauna, including rare and endangered species.

Hovsgol Lake is a major water source for the Selenge River.

The lake feeds into the Selenge River, which is one of the main tributaries of Lake Baikal. This makes Hovsgol Lake a vital water source not only for the local ecosystem but also for the surrounding regions.

It is a haven for bird enthusiasts.

Hovsgol Lake attracts a wide variety of bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. Over 200 species of birds have been recorded in the area, including the elegant Swan Goose and the majestic White-naped Crane.

The lake is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

In 2011, Hovsgol Lake and its surrounding areas were designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its exceptional natural value and significant cultural heritage.

Hovsgol Lake holds significant scientific value.

Scientists and researchers are drawn to Hovsgol Lake due to its unique characteristics and ecological importance. They study the lake to gain insights into climate change, biodiversity, and the intricate interactions between ecosystems.
