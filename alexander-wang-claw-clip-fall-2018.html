Alexander Wang Just Brought Back The Claw Clip

 Posted on July 11, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  548 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Nothing is cool until Rihanna or Alexander Wang says so. Case in point: at the latter's '80s-themed fall 2018 show, models walked the runway wearing a claw clip (!) in their hair. But it wasn't a plain black plastic or tortoiseshell clip that you could pick-up at CVS tomorrow—it was finished in chrome silver and stamped with Wang's logo.

Harper's Bazaar

If you aren't sure how to feel about the accessory's return—you're not alone. While some women still swear by the clips to hold ultra-thick hair, it hasn't been seen in high-fashion circles since Friends was still on the air. But since the humble scrunchie made its triumphant return to fashion and celebrity culture last year, anything is possible.

"The hair is take on a power executive of the '80s. It's corporate and kind of smart in a way," says lead hairstylist and Redken creative director Guido Palau. "This is Alex's take on the ironic banana clip of that era." Palau and his team twisted the hair up along the center of the head, then secured it with pins and one or two Wang-branded clips. A blast of Redken Forceful 23 hairspray secured the "very dynamic and tough" look.

Harper's Bazaar

We asked Palau about his personal thoughts on the clip. "I love the way that something that's been around and has a funny connotation can come back. Look at the mullet haircut: three or four years ago it was a no-no, and now it's super cool. I love the way that something is reintroduced into fashion and we look at it again in a different way. That's been happening for a while now, things that were considered not cool or a bit of can be rejigged again."

If you remember the clips from the first time around—and perhaps still have a few—Palau wants to make you feel really, really old: "Also, you've got to remember, lots of girls probably don't remember what the banana clip is. There's an 18 or 17-year-old girl, they don't know it has a funny connotation to it. They may just think it's super cool to do that in their hair."

No word yet on whether or not Wang will sell the clips come fall. Hairstylists were handling the plastic accessories backstage with the grace of a ballerina carrying a newborn (only a handful were made for the show—so if they broke, they broke). If they do find their way into stories in a few months, we can imagine they'll be right alongside the Tom Ford leather headbands as a must-have item of the season.
