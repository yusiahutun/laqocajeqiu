Love Island: The Morning After podcast host Sam Thompson has been in the limelight for a decade.

The 30-year-old TV personality shot to fame on E4's Made in Chelsea back in 2013.

He has since become a reality TV regular appearing on Celebrity Coach Trip, Celebs Go Dating and Celebrity Circle.

Sam is the younger brother of Made in Chelsea legend Louise Thompson.

He lives next door to Louise and her fiancé Ryan Libbey and their son Leo in Chelsea SW1.

The TV star previously opened up about "nearly losing" Louise after she had a traumatic birth.

She suffered various complications and spent a month in ICU with her Leo spent time in NICU.

He said at the time: ""Nearly losing my sister was one of the toughest things I’ve ever had to deal with.

"The hardest situations teach the biggest lessons and I learnt to never take any day for granted ever again."

Co-star 'feud'

Sam reportedly fell out with Made in Chelsea co-star Jamie Laing after the former Strictly Come Dancing contestant started dating his ex Sophie 'Habbs' Habboo.

However, Sam quickly shut down the claims and told OK!: "Honestly, I was only going out with her [Sophie] for a very short amount of time. I think that it’s a whole Made in Chelsea thing, it’s not to do with me.

"My name keeps getting brought up though, I was getting Google alerts about it. I haven’t even said anything about it either, as I don’t really think there’s much to say on my part. It’s got nothing really to do with me."

Love Island star girlfriend

Former Love Island beauty Zara McDermott went Instagram official with Sam back in 2019.

They broke up after 16 months after Zara reportedly cheated on Sam.

After the model issued a grovelling apology, Sam took Zara back and the pair seem stronger than ever.

Sam previously revealed that he's ready to get engaged but Zara is stalling it.

He told Closer recently: "I get loads of questions about a ring on her finger.

"It’s not actually me, it’s her. She’s like 'I’m not ready, I’m working on this, I’m doing that.' And she’s so right.

"We both know we want to be with each other. I know she’s going to say yes, well she says she’s going to say yes when I ask."

Jaw-dropping net worth

Sam started working as an intern for "rival" Jamie Laing at his Candy Kittens business.

His exact net worth isn't known but experts have estimated he's worth around a whopping £850,000 and £1million.

The bubbly TV personality has one of the most in-demand stars in recent time and offered appears as a guest on panel TV shows and podcasts.
