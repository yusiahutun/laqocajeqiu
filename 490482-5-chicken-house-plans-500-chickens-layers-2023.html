Constructing a chicken house for less than 20 chickens requires no special layout. The entire process involves erecting one house. However, it becomes more demanding when you want a structure to house more than 500 chickens. In this case, you must pay special consideration to the placement of the building that will facilitate proper feeding, protection of the birds from the weather, disease control, and ease and economical operation. You can get inspiration from free chicken house plans for 500 chickens.

With many chicken house plans for 500 chickens, especially for layers, it becomes challenging to determine which one you want. Generally, a layer house is one in which you can rear chickens over 18 weeks up to 72 weeks of age.

Since you have 500 chickens, look for a plan with several cages to breed up several hens. Also, it may be helpful to determine whether you need a plan with breeding equipment or feeding devices. But which plans should you choose?

5 best chicken house plans for 500 chickens (layers) in 2023

You can get inspiration from unlimited-layer chicken house plans. However, if the plans do not have all the requirements you need, you may have to adjust them accordingly.

One-layer chicken only requires two square feet of space. Therefore, you need 1,000 square feet to build a chicken house for 500 chickens. Nevertheless, you should consider these five chicken house plans for 500 chickens.

1. Agico chicken house plan

The Agico chicken house plan is the best poultry house plan for 500 chickens. It is a three-tier layer cage with four sets that can host up to 144 birds per set. Thus, you can use it to house more than 500 chickens. Also, the house measures 10 × 4 × 3 metres, making it easier for you to walk in and attend to your chickens.

The Agico chicken house plan is suitable for small-scale laying hen production. Although it has a small breeding volume, it addresses the issue by arranging the house in a single row. The total length of the four groups of layer cages arranged in a single row is 7.33 metres.

It reserves some space for operations and equipment places. But the plan falls short, whereby it does not have automatic auxiliary breeding equipment. Therefore, you will have to add cleaning, water, and feeding devices.

2. The Birchwood

The Birchwood is a chicken house plan for layers that suits the requirements of up to 500 free-range birds. It has a unique design built on three metal skids, meaning you can move it to a different location if necessary. However, you also have the option of building the structure without skids.

The house measures 24' x 14' plus a 4' egg packing room. The Birchwood has a Tama slatted floor perching area that hooks up and is removable to allow easy cleaning. Also, its main house has a solid wooden floor to enable the birds to scratch.

It also includes nest boxes that you can close manually or automatically at night. Other features included in the design are windows an access door.

3. The Needwood

The Needwood is a simple poultry house in three sizes that can house between 100 and 300 chickens. It is suitable for layers and homeowners that want to engage in commercial egg-laying activities. But because of its elegant design, you can tweak its measurements to accommodate 500 chickens.

It has an internal floor area of 36 square metres. Thus, you can adjust it to 90 square meters, enough to house over 500 chickens. The chicken house has metal roll-away nest boxes that help keep eggs clean. Also, it features metal towing skids to allow you to move the structure from one area to another.

The inside of The Needwood has a large bank of perches for birds. You can hook the perches up to give you room to clean the house. Also, the building has sliding windows to allow for light and ventilation.

4. The Charnwood

The Charnwood is a traditional wooden chicken house with an internal area of 35 square meters. Although the size can house 300 chickens, its flexible design makes it easier to adjust the structure and accommodate 500 chickens.

It has 30 nests, but you can add many more to make it easier for more hens to lay their eggs in a clean cubicle. The Charnwood features traditional nest boxes on the front. It also has perches with droppings board below it to make it easier to clean and collect waste.

The interior is high enough to walk in and do other activities like collecting eggs. And if the chickens want light, the house has sliding glass windows that offer light and ventilation.

5. Homegardendesignplan L200

The L200 Chicken Coop Plan from Homegardendesignplan is one of this list's most detailed chicken house plans. Although the plan has 20 nesting birds, ideal for 100 birds, you can adjust the poultry house design measurements to accommodate up to 500 chickens.
