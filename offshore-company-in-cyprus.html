Understanding the Structure of an Offshore Company in Cyprus

When establishing a company in Cyprus several crucial factors must be carefully considered.

1. Transparency

Information about directors, shareholders and financial statements is publicly accessible. However details about the owner are kept confidential. Nominee services provide anonymity for Directors and Shareholders.

2. Shares

An Offshore Company can issue types of shares like registered shares, preference shares, redeemable shares and shares with or without voting rights.

3. Capitalization

While there is no minimum capital requirement, the usual authorized capital amounts, to €1,000. Financial Reporting RequirementsIt is necessary to create reports. They are not shared with the public.

Staffing Requirements

A business should have at one Director of any nationality, than Cypriot, who can be an individual or a legal entity. Additionally a Secretary must be designated, who can also serve as the Director if the ownership is single.

1. Shareholders

For a Cyprus company there should be a minimum of one shareholder and a maximum of fifty. Shareholders can belong to any nationality than Cypriot. May be individuals or corporate entities.

2. Currency Control

Cyprus does not impose any restrictions on currency exchange.

3. Language and Official Papers

Greek is the language for laws and corporate paperwork along with an English translation.

4. Official Address

Every Cyprus offshore company must have a registered office.

5. Auditing and Reporting

Financial statements must undergo audits. Be submitted to the Cyprus Inland Revenue Department (IRD). Financial reports need to be prepared in accordance with IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards). Submitted to the Cyprus Tax Authorities.

6. Company Naming Rules

An offshore company needs authorization for activities, like banking, insurance and financial services.
