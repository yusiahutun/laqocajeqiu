Courtney Thorne-Smith's biography

The actress was born on November 8, 1967, in San Francisco, California. She grew up in a suburb in the south of San Francisco known as Menlo Park. Her father (Walter Smith) is a computer market researcher, and her mother (Lora Thorne) is a therapist.

When Courtney was seven years old, her parents divorced. As a result, young Courtney had a double life living with both parents at different stages of her life. The star has an elder sister, Jennifer Smith, who is an advertising executive.

Courtney attended Menlo-Atherton High School in Atherton, California. Later, she went to Tamalpais High School in Mill Valley, California, where she graduated in 1985. While in school, she was an active performer with the Ensemble Theater Company in Mill Valley.

  • Welcome to 18 (1986)

In the mid-1980s, Courtney appeared in a few television series. Her first notable role was as Alison Parker on Melrose Place from 1992 to 1997. Later, she starred in a made-for-television movie, Beauty's Revenge, in 1995, marketed in CD and DVD form as Midwest Obsession.

The movie was a great success leading her to play Georgia Thomas on Ally McBeal from 1997 to 2000. Initially, Thorne-Smith auditioned for the title role in the show, which was unfortunately given to Calista Flockhart. At the beginning of season four of the show in 2000, her character was written out, but she returned for occasional guest appearances.

Besides acting, Thorne is a writer. She published her first novel, Outside In, on September 18, 2007. The book was published by Broadway Books in New York.
