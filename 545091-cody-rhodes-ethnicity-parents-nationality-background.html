Who is Cody Rhodes?

Cody Rhodes was born on 30 June 1985 in Marietta, Georgia, United States. His parents are Virgil Riley Runnels Jr. (father) and Michelle Rubio (mother). What nationality is Cody Rhodes? Code is an American national of mixed ethnic background.

His father was also an iconic professional wrestler, booker, and trainer, while his mother is a former dancer and receptionist. Virgil died of renal failure on 11 June 2015. The American celebrity grew up alongside his sister, Teil Margaret Runnels Gergel.

Cody also has two half-siblings, Dustin Patrick Runnels and Kristin Runnels Ditto, from his father's first marriage. Teil is a teacher based in Texas, while Patrick (Goldust) is a professional wrestler widely recognised for his multiple tenures with WWE between 1995 and 2018.

Patrick is currently signed with All Elite Wrestling under the ring name Dustin Rhodes. Kristin is an entrepreneur and the founder of a real estate venture called KDreamLiving. Cody attended Lassiter High School.

What is Cody Rhodes' ethnicity?

What is Cody Rhodes' race? Cody Rhodes has a mixed-racial background. He is of white descent from his father's side and Spanish-Cuban descent from his mother's side.

Is Cody Rhodes Hispanic?

Yes, Cody Rhodes is half-Hispanic since his mother is of a Spanish-Cuban ethnic background. However, the former AEW star revealed in an interview in March 2024 that he is always reluctant to disclose that he is half-Cuban since his fans may be upset.

Early professional career

Cody Rhodes began training for wrestling in 1997 when he was 12. He was later trained by legendary wrestlers like Randy Orton, Al Snow, and Danny Davis. He made his professional debut on 13 May 2006, performing as Cody Runnels with Ohio Valley Wrestling.

On 16 July 2007, he made his WWE Raw in-ring debut as Rhodes. In November 2008, he became part of The Legacy group with Orton and Ted DiBiase.

As part of the 2010 WWE supplemental draft, the Marietta native was drafted to the SmackDown brand. He debuted for the brand in April of the same year by defeating John Morrison. Later, Rhodes formed a tag team with his brother, Patrick, who was then performing as Goldust.

Cody Rhodes' departure from World Wrestling Entertainment

The American entertainer left World Wrestling Entertainment in 2016. Rhodes disclosed during his interview on Wrestledelphia Radio that his father influenced his decision to leave TKO Group Holdings' promotion. He said,

At 28, I recalled particularly, he was unhappy with the direction things were going with me and Big Show, and he just thought that I was so close to the keys to the kingdom, and they were slipping away, that maybe leaving would wake them to the idea that this is somebody who wanted to captain your ship, and the way you guys are treating him has pushed him out.

After Rhode left WWE, he pursued a career on the independent professional wrestling circuit, performing in promotions such as Ring of Honor, Impact Wrestling, New Japan Pro Wrestling, and All Elite Wrestling. He competed using in-ring names like Cody, Cody R, and The American Dream.

Cody Rhodes' return to World Wrestling Entertainment

The American star was re-signed to World Wrestling Entertainment in early 2022. He defeated Seth Freakin Rollins on 2 April 2022 on the WWE's first night of WrestleMania 38. He eventually became the Undisputed World Wrestling Entertainment Universal Champion after he won against Roman Reigns on 8 April 2024 at WrestleMania 40.

Rhodes is also an accomplished actor. He is widely recognised for starring as Derek Sampson and Kurt Smoller in the TV series Arrow and Warehouse 13, respectively. According to his IMDb page, Rhodes has approximately 81 acting credits under his name.
