How to prevent scalp burn from relaxer

 Posted on July 14, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1223 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Scalp burn from relaxer is quite common, especially if your hairdresser does not observe the instructions about time, written on the package of the chemical. So, why do people use chemical hair relaxer, in spite of the glaring danger of scalp burns? Well, chemically relaxing your hair leads to a shiny, straight hair. It increases the beauty of your hair and makes it easy for you to style it with a little application of hair oils.

Even though the benefits of applying hair relaxer are quite apparent, its side effects are just bad. Scalp burn is one side effect of applying chemical hair relaxer. However, there are ways to ensure this does not happen. Some of these measures are to be taken, before, during and after application of the chemical. This is to help with itchy scalp before relaxer; scalp burn from relaxer, and scalp still burning after relaxer.

What is a scalp burn?

Scalp burn is a burn that occurs on the scalp when chemical hair relaxer is applied and left to cook for a set period. It often begins with a tingling sensation on the scalp and advances to a more severe burn when the chemical is left to sit on the head for long. To some, these effects occur pretty quick, especially to those people with a sensitive scalp, while to others, it might take longer.

Luckily, there are preventive measures that you can take to ensure your scalp is protected from the adverse effects of chemical burn from relaxer. Here are some of them.

1. Apply a base cream on your hairline and scalp

Before applying the hair relaxer, ensure that your hairdresser has applied a base cream all around the head, and the ears. If you have sensitive skin, you have to ensure that s/he applies a base on your entire scalp. S/he should use his or her fingers to apply the base cream on the hairline.

Some people do not like the idea of applying a base on their scalp and around the head. However, this should not stop you from doing the right thing and protecting yourself against the harsh effects of hair relaxer.

2. Do not exercise two days before the day of the application

Exercising is a good way to maintain an active lifestyle. It also results in sweating, which opens up your skin pores. Open skin pores pre-exposes you to a higher likelihood of being burned during the application of a hair relaxer.

3. Do not wash your hair before relaxing

It is prudent to shampoo your hair at least a week before relaxing it. This is to allow your scalp enough rest before applying the relaxer. In the same vein, do not wet or cleanse your hair before relaxing it. All of these will lead to burning.

Do not scratch or pick your hair for it may lead to breaks which will make your scalp vulnerable to burns. You have to ensure that your scalp has every advantage it can against burns when applying the hair relaxer.

4. Do not apply relaxer on a head with sores

Avoid applying hair relaxer on your head if it has sores and irritation. Wait until the scalp has healed completely before applying the relaxer. And after the application of a relaxer, refrain from scratching an itchy scalp as this might lead to wounds. Instead, rub the itchy part with a lot of care, or gently until you feel better.

5. Keep time

More often than not, hairdressers attend to other issues while they wait for your hair to cook well. This might distract them from your case, and in turn, lead to burns as a result of extended time. As a client, always keep track of time. And prompt your hairdresser as soon as the recommended time has elapsed. This will ensure everything goes as is supposed to.

6. Use a soft toothed comb

When combing your hair a week before the scheduled date for hair relaxing, use a soft toothed comb. This will protect you against the possibility of breaking your scalp, which might open it up for chemical penetration.

During the application of the hair relaxer, you should ensure that the hairdresser is not using the teeth of the comb to smooth the relaxer on your hair, for this might lead to scratching and wounding your scalp. Instead, s/he should use the back of the comb. Better yet, s/he should use their hands to spread the relaxer all over your hair.

7. Use the right relaxer

You have to ensure that your hairdresser is not using just about any type of hair relaxer on you. Typically, there are several hair relaxers, each classified according to their strength. There are super, mild, and regular hair relaxers. You have to ensure that you know the type of relaxer that is best suited for you, and confirm that indeed, the hair relaxer being used by the hairdresser, is right for your hair.

8. Let your hairdresser know you

Before s/he can start working on you, tell the hairdresser, the type of client you are - whether sensitive or not. This will give him or her an idea of the amount of time to spend on you. And if you feel a tingling sensation or an actual burn on your scalp, speak up so that immediate action can be taken. If the burn is from a certain of your head, the hairdresser will apply a certain oil sheen on that area to quell the situation.

9. Rinse

After the treatment, rinse your hair in a continuously flowing water. Preferably, rinse your hair in a sink or a bath tab to get off all the chemical on your hair. In case, you feel a tingling sensation during the application; you need to wash all the chemical off your hair immediately. This is to avoid any possible burns from occurring.

It is also important to know how to treat scalp burns from relaxers. In case you get burns after you apply relaxer, you have to ensure that your hairdresser does not spray any product with alcohol on your scalp as this will worsen the burn. Instead, s/he should apply petroleum jelly on the damaged scalp from relaxer, because it will also help the scab to fall off more easily.

Apart from base cream for relaxers, there are other preventive measures that you can take to prevent scalp burn from relaxer. All you need to do is to apply them. One thing though, you have to ensure that you visit the right hairdresser. One who understands the concept of hair relaxers and the need to use it wisely. Always keep safe while pursuing beauty, because everyone needs you healthy more than they need you beautiful.
