Showjumping protg, 20, is 'finally in a good place' after trainer, 63, groomed her

 Posted on June 20, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  951 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

A showjumping protégé who was groomed and sexually assaulted by her Olympic trainer is 'finally in a good place' after years of abuse, her mother has told DailyMail.com.

Millionaire equestrian star Richard Fellers, 63, was jailed on Tuesday after admitting to abusing Maggie Kehring, 20, when he was training her.

Fellers, who was handed a four-year federal jail term and 30-month state sentence, first met Maggie when she was 14 and she idolized him because of his horsemanship and skill.

He confessed he had feelings for the young rider when she was 16, and took her virginity when she was just 17.

Maggie's mother Carrie, who has since founded non-profit We Ride Together to address sexual abuse in equestrian sport, said her daughter is 'finally in a good place'.

She said: 'For a few years she really struggled to get herself back and what had happened was endlessly on her mind.

'She's worked so hard to find herself again, she doesn't want to give it space in her brain at this time.'

Carrie added there have been at least eight trainers arrested in the past eight months on suspicion of abuse within the riding community.

Feller's abuse of Maggie continued until June 2020 when his wife, Shelley, walked in on them together at a show in Michigan. She has since filed for divorce earlier this year.

Shelley has also been suspended from competitive equestrianism for failing to report the abuse, something which she is appealing.

In a statement following his sentencing, Maggie said: 'I have no comment on the Rich Fellers case. The public record speaks for itself.

'Right now, I send my thoughts to those victims of sexual abuse navigating the very emotional and difficult legal process.'

The horseman, who represented the US in the London 2012 Olympics, pleaded guilty to interstate travel to engage in illicit sexual conduct with a minor. His sentences will run concurrently.

Maggie said initially she was in awe of him, after watching him win the 2012 World Cup on TV, but told CBS that she now sees him as a 'sociopath' following the abuse.

Allegations of abuse first came to light in February 2021 when Fellers and his wife were added to the U.S. Center for SafeSport's list of temporary suspensions.

Feller was arrested in Jun 2021 on suspicion of sexually abusing Maggie at her apartment in Portland, with his suspension being made for life.

By the time Maggie was 15 she had moved into a small apartment near Feller's barn in Oregon – leaving her family behind in California.

In December 2019 she got a call from Fellers, who was 60 at the time, who told her 'I'm crazy about you' – with Maggie saying at the time she was 'filled with fear' and cried.

He reportedly told her she was a 'goddess', and he would touch her as they had dinner with his wife and two children.

After Shelley walked in on the pair, Kehring returned home, where she posted videos of herself sobbing with photos of her and Fellers.

The publicity meant that more than a dozen people reported Fellers to the U.S. Center for SafeSport, which was set up in the wake of the abuse of gymnasts by Larry Nassar.

But the teenager 'spiraled into depression' and took six months to come to terms with what happened before eventually speaking to SafeSport over the allegations.

Her attorney Russell Prince added: 'For all the horrible people in the equestrian community that said terrible things about Maggie, I think there can be no clearer vindication for what she's been through [than this].

'There's quite a few people who owe Maggie Kehring and the Kehring family some heartfelt apologies.'

Fellers, who runs a large equestrian operation in Oregon City, was given a horse for his 11th birthday and soon established himself as a local champion.

In 1989 he bought the stable yard outside Portland where he is now based, which is currently valued at $1.8 million.

He first represented the United States in 1991, when he was part of the bronze-winning U.S. showjumping team at the Pan-American Games in Havana, Cuba.

Fellers came sixth in the London 2012 Olympics on Flexible, a 16-year-old stallion who he began riding in 2002.

At the time, Shelly Fellers praised her husband of 25 years as 'a great husband, great partner'.

She added: 'He hates being away from his kids and the family.' They have two adult children, Christopher and Savannah.
