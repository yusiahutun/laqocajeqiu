List of flowers that are native to Africa with pictures

 Posted on May 6, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1325 words  |  Martina Birk

Africa is the second-largest continent on the planet and is home to millions of plant species. Most of the vegetation cover grows wildly and are yet to be studied. This is the case for the African flowering plants, most of which grow in forested and remote areas. Flowers that are native to Africa are world renown for their beauty and diversity.

Africa is home to beautiful flowering plants that are not only unique but also have different uses as well. Countries like South Africa have become popular destinations for tourists and scientists who are enthusiasts of African flower names and meanings.

10 flowers that are native to Africa

Discovering the fantastic diversity and the beauty of Africa is a journey of intrigue and marvel. Here is a list of the top 10 flowers that are native to Africa with their pictures:

1. Geissorhiza tulbaghensis

The Geissorhiza tulbaghensis flowering plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and typically grows from a bulb or cormlet. It is also known as the Tulbagh satin flower. It has become popular as a rock garden flower and is sometimes planted in pots.

The flower is native to the Western Cape and is among the most popular South African flowers. The plant's colours vary from shades of white, purple to brown. Its blooming period is usually towards the end of winter as the spring starts when its deep-green slender leaves and large blossoms stand out.

2. Strelitzia reginae

The Strelitzia reginae is a flowering plant that originates from South Africa. It is a member of the Strelitziaceae family and the order Zingiberales. The perennial plant was first introduced to Europe in the year 1773 after which it was spread to other parts of the world.

The low-maintenance plant blooms in autumn, winter and spring. It has the potential to grow up to six feet high and has large banana-like leaves that measure about 28 inches long. The flower sprouts from a beak-like casing known as the spathe and joins the stem at a right angle making the flower to appear like a bird's head.

3. Spathodea campanulata

Spathodea campanulate is one of the most spectacular flowering plants. It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and order lamiales. It has bronze-coloured leaves that grow to about two feet long. Its buds are horn-shaped and form in large clusters at the tips of its branches.

The lowest and outermost buds of the plant flare up into red-orange or yellow tulip-like bells with crinkled margins. The flowers grow to about 10 cm long and are ever-present because the plant flowers all-year-round.

4. Adenium obesum

Adenium obesum is an African wildflower that belongs to the family Apocynaceae and order Gentianales. It is native to the Southern, tropical, subtropical Africa, and the Middle East. It is drought-tolerant and grows to about three meters in height. It flowers in winter and lasts until summer.

Its thick trunk and woody base is called the caudex. The tree has leathery leaves that clump together at the tips of its branches in a spiral arrangement. The Adenium obesum's flowers have the shape of a bell with five petals that are about 5cm in length. The colour of the flower is usually pink or red with a whitish flush in the middle.

5. Protea cynaroides

The Protea cynaroides is a flowering plant that is a member of the family Proteaceae and order Proteales. It is native to the southern and southwestern parts of South Africa. Its name was derived from the Greek god Proteus, the god who changes shape at his will.

The flower is found in different forms. Protea cynaroides is a shrub that has stout stems and dark-green leaves. The flowers are found at the head of the plant and are surrounded by colourful bracts that vary from light pink to a deep crimson.

6. Gloriosa superba

Gloriosa superba is a sizeable African flower that stems from the tuberous herb that is part of the family Colchicaceae and order Liliales. It is found all over the tropical and southern parts of Africa and regions of tropical Asia. It derives its name from the bright red and yellow colour of the flowers.

The name Gloriosa is borrowed from the word glorious, which means good-looking. Superba is derived from superb to mean splendid. The flower grows best in sandy-loam soils and sunny conditions.

It has wrinkled and elongated flowers with prominent stamens that disperse outwards in long curves. The length of the flower is about 5 cm long, and its leaves are bright green. The entire plant has alkaloids that make it too toxic to ingest.

7. Aloe arborescens

Aloe arborescens is a perennial flowering plant that blooms during winter. The plant is a member of the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and order Asparagales. Its origin is in the southeastern parts of South Africa, and it has a potential of growing up to 3 meters high.

The flowers of the plant have a raceme arrangement and have a cylindrical shape. The colour of the flower varies from orange to bright red. The leaves are long with small spikes around the edges. The other names of the plant are candelabra aloe and krantz aloe.

8. Arctotis

This African flower species are part of the family Asteraceae and is sometimes referred to as the African Daisy. Its flowers have a daisy-like appearance that makes them spectacular. They also appear in a variety of colours including orange, creamy, pale yellow and more.

The flower is black at the centre, and the flowers form rings around it. The Arctotis blooms between spring and summer. The flowers tend to close late in the afternoon or when the weather is dull.

9. Carpobrotus edulis

The origin of the Carpobrotus edulis is believed to be in the coastal regions of South Africa. The family name of the plant is Aizoaceae, and it belongs to order Caryophyllales. It has large flowers that are daisy-like and vary in colour from purple, red, yellow to dark pink.

The blooming season of Carpobrotus edulis is in late winter-spring. It is a perennial plant with succulent leaves which have medicinal value and are used as an antiseptic for sore throat treatment. Some of its other names are ice plant, pigface and highway ice plant.

10. Dimorphotheca pluvialis

Dimorphotheca Pluvialis belongs to Asteraceae family and Asterales order. Its origins are in South-West Africa. Its height is approximately 40 centimetres. It blooms between mid-spring and mid-summer.

The two characterizations of the flowers are disc florets and ray florets. The disc florets are often yellowish and have purplish markings. The ray florets are white to yellowish and have purple to blue markings. The flowering plant has fleshy succulent long narrow leaves that can survive dry conditions.
