Love Island star Tasha Ghouri's dad hits out at Molly-Mae copy cat claims

 Posted on March 3, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  487 words  |  Martina Birk

Love Island's Tasha Ghouri's dad has quashed claims that his daughter is copying former star, Molly-Mae Hague.

The 23-year-old dancer has been spotted around the villa carrying around her 'Flat Ted' toy, similar to the one people saw in 2019 with Molly's teddy called Ellie Belly.

Although people were quick to make the comparison, Tasha's dad has jumped in to defend claims that she's copying the ex-islander.

Fans have seen Tasha sweetly cuddling 'Flat Ted' around the villa and he even made an appearance during a raunchy night in the hideaway with her partner Andrew Le Page.

When viewers saw Molly Mae snuggling with her cuddly toy back in 2019, it made her seem endearing to people watching at home and gained her some popularity.

People are now accusing Tasha and her 'Flat Ted' as a ploy to try and win over the public similar to Molly's Elly Belly.

However, her dad has since come out to defend his daughter as he stated Tasha "does not copy anyone."

Replying to an Instagram post, he wrote: "Hi! Tashas Dad here. I can tell you flat ted has been EVERYWHERE with Tash and our family since she was 1.

"He’s travelled the world and spent every single night with her. He’s been to these places more than once: America, Mexico, Greece, Spain, France, Finland, Sweden, Bulgaria, Balearics.

"The list goes on. He’s been in cockpits of planes and even met famous people. He has provided comfort on those nights as a kid she needed it. He’s given her support when she needed it.

The post continued: "I love flat ted as much as Tash does. Tash does not Copy anyone. She is her own natural self and took Flat Ted with her because he is always with her and there's no reason he should not be by her side in this experience.

"She can't talk or communicate to us so I'm happy flat ted is by her side."

Her dad's defence comes after people quickly compared her toy to Molly's after watching the show.

One person on Twitter said: "Tasha really thought she’d be creating an iconic moment like this with her tatty teddy."

Another said: “Tasha stop babe your whole teddy thing is a bit beggy we’ve already seen that season lol.”

Someone else commented: "Not Tasha trying to be Molly mae and Tommy with the teddy”.
