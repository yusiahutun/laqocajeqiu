THE Daytime Emmy Awards this year did not disappoint, honoring stars and shows like no one's business.

All My Children star Susan Lucci was honored this year with the Lifetime Achievement Award for her 40-year run as Erica Kane on the ABC show.

"How lucky was I to be put in the hands of (creator) Agnes Nixon?" she said after being presented with the award.

"Agnes was the trailblazer. She was the visionary storyteller who gave me the part of a lifetime: The fabulously flawed Erica Kane."

The actress also thanked her son, Andreas Huber, and late husband, Helmut Huber, saying: "It means so much to me to have [Andreas] here with me.

"And you know what else? I feel your dad's presence here tonight with us, too. I thank my husband, Helmut Huber, because he has everything to do with my standing up here tonight, and receiving this incredible award."

Meanwhile, General Hospital won big, taking home six awards, including one for the late Sonya Eddy in her role as head nurse Epiphany Johnson, who died last December following a post-op infection.

  • Barbara Walters tribute at the awards

    Connie Chung, a fellow news anchor, honored Barbara Walters, a pioneer who, among her many achievements, founded ABC's popular daytime talk show The View in 1997.

    Walters was 93 when she passed away in December of last year. As "the creator, the producer, the star – the engine behind it," Walters "revolutionized the face of daytime television," according to Chung.

    "And this was on top of her pioneering career in news and interviews. She was the icon who paved the way for all women in television news, especially me. She mom-ed me.

    "And although they would never admit it, she taught the men a thing or two, I can’t fathom she is gone. We remember her and all the others who passed, for their contributions to television.”

  • Christopher's career in a nutshell

    Tyler Christopher was a popular soap opera actor who was born on November 11, 1972, in Joliet, Illinois.

    He got his start in the entertainment industry back in the 1990s when he landed the role of Nikolas Cassadine on General Hospital.

    The Daytime Emmy Award winner portrayed the role of Nikolas, and later Connor Bishop, in over 1,100 episodes between 1996 and 2016.

    Between 2001 and 2019, he also starred as Stefan DiMera in 155 episodes of Days of Our Lives.

    Christopher's resume also includes roles in the films Raven (2010), Pretty Broken (2018), Super Volcano (2022), and Ice Storm (2023).

    Throughout his career, Christopher amassed a net worth of $7 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.

  • Tyler Christopher's brush with the law

    On May 26, 2023, Christopher was taken into custody and charged with public intoxication after police confronted him at Burbank airport.

    Sources told TMZ that cops found him sleeping on the ground near the American Airlines terminal.

    Christopher reportedly told the officers that he had missed his flight but had "displayed clear and objective signs of intoxication" and was "unable to care for himself," according to TMZ.

    The soap star was ultimately cited and released on $250 bail but was expected to appear back in court at a later date, according to the Los Angeles Times.

    Christopher was also charged with public intoxication in November 2019 after he fell asleep in the back of an Uber, the Los Angeles Times notes.

    For that charge, he pleaded guilty to two counts of public intoxication, but details on the resolution of the case are unclear at this time.

  • More on Sonya Eddy

    Sonya Eddy was an actress who played a series of roles across genres but was best known for her role as head nurse Epiphany Johnson on the soap opera General Hospital.

    The actress was a crucial figure in the show for more than 16 years and was in 543 episodes.

    She also was featured in other movies and shows, including The Drew Carey Show, Seinfeld, Patch Adams, Reba, Monk, Joan of Arcadia, CSI, Glee, Castle, and Fresh Off The Boat.

  • General hospital star Sonya Eddy and her role

    Sonya Eddy played Epiphany Johnson, the head nurse at General Hospital.

    Sonya had her LPN license, meaning she was a Licensed Practical Nurse.

    On the show, Epiphany was known for being outspoken and willing to speak up for others.

    Epiphany notably corrected other nurses and even doctors on more than one occasion.

    She also plays the mother to the late character Stan Johnson, who worked for and was later killed by the mob.

  • Sonya Eddy's recent death

    Tyler Ford, a close friend of Sonya's, disclosed her cause of death to TMZ on Wednesday. December 21.

    According to Ford, Sonya reported to the hospital on December 9 for scheduled surgery, after which she was sent home on December 11.

    Sonya then returned to the hospital on December 15, when doctors discovered that she was ill with a severe infection from a complication of the surgery.

    Ford said that he learned from hospital staff that his friend had been put on life support on Monday morning, December 19.

    She died later that evening.
