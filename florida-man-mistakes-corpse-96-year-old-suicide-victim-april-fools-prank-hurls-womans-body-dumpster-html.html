Florida man mistakes corpse for April Fool's prank and dumps it in the trash

Florida man mistakes corpse of 96-year-old suicide victim for April Fool's prank and hurls the woman's body into a dumpster

April Fool's Day pranks are often known to be macabre, so when a Florida man spotted what appeared to be a mannequin made to look like a corpse outside his work Tuesday, he tossed it in the trash without giving it a second thought.

It was not until police arrived hours later that 61-year-old Ronald Benjamin, of St Petersburg, learned that the prop he had hurled into the Dumpster was actually a deceased 96-year-old woman.  

The victim jumped to her death from the 16th floor of the Peterborough Apartments senior housing complex where she lived, landing on the pavement outside 440 Fourth Avenue North.

No laughing matter: A memorial sits outside the Peterborough Apartments in St. Petersburg, Florida, where a 96-year-old woman was mistaken for a dummy and thrown in the garbage after she jumped to her death out of a 16th-story apartment

That is where Benjamin, who was manning the front desk in the apartment building, came upon the body when he went out for a smoke at around 4.30am.

The shapeless form on the ground resembled a mannequin, and Benjamin concluded that a reveler returning from a bar must have left it there as a ghoulish April Fool's joke, Tampa Bay Times reported.

Two hours later, another staffer at the housing complex raised concerns about the human figure on the patio, but Benjamin assured her that it was fake.

When a deliverywoman and her son came by later to drop off newspapers, Benjamin asked the boy to help him dispose of the 'dummy' with a knot of gray hair on its head.

Benjamin later recalled that the figure was very light as he and the boy tossed it into a garbage bin.
The clerk did notice that there was some blood on the ground, but he dismissed it as fake gore.

At around 8.30am, a maintenance worker opened the Dumpster to discover the body of a 96-year-old woman, who had committed suicide overnight.

The news left Mr Benjamin stunned and unable to sleep. He insisted that the victim’s face looked rubbery, just like a Halloween mask.

Macabre: Desk clerk Ronald Benjamin was working the night shift at the senior housing complex on Fourth Avenue North when he spotted what he believed to be a mannequin left by an April Fool's Day prankster

'If I thought for one instant it was a real person I would have called the police, my manager, everyone I could think of,' Benjamin stated.

The man is not expected to face any charges, but he was fired in the aftermath of the incident after nine years on the job.   

The suicide victim was reportedly a native of England who came to the U.S. after World War II.  

Her neighbors described the 96-year-old as bright and well-read with the mental acuity of a person half her age.

An investigation has revealed that the elderly resident was suffering from depression, the station WTSP reported. She left a suicide note before plummeting to her death from her 16th-floor balcony.

According to detectives, Benjamin may have mistaken the victim's face for a mask because it had been distorted by rigor mortis by the time he found her.
