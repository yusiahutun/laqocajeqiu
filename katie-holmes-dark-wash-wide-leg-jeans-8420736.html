Katie Holmes Reminded Us That Big Pants Arent Going Anywhere in 2024

 Posted on July 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  553 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

I’ve said it before and I’ll say it again: Katie Holmes just knows how to nail street style. Her looks are always effortless yet polished, and she manages to incorporate at least one major trend without fail, whether she’s wearing loafers, metallic heels, or Mary Janes. While her footwear game is on-point, she also knows a thing or two about good bottoms, and her recent outfit is proof.

Holmes stepped out in New York City wearing a totally chic ensemble I instantly wanted to recreate. She layered a basic black turtleneck under a long textured coat that was adorned with large gold buttons. A striking red dumpling bag hung across her chest, and sleek black leather heels created the ideal ‘fit from head to toe — but I’d be doing you a disservice if I didn’t highlight her billowy wide-leg jeans.

Spanx Wide-Leg Jeans

Frame Le Slim Palazzo High-Waist Wide-Leg Jeans

Her style of bottoms are nothing new; big, baggy pants have been taking over the fashion scene since 2022 with Jennifer Lawrence, Chrissy Teigen, Gwyneth Paltrow, and Jennifer Lopez all donning the style. This look is a direct contradiction to the classic skinny jean, and it’s safe to say the oversized trend will seep into 2024 — honestly, I’m more than excited about its continuity.

Wide-leg jeans, like these Spanx options, elongate the leg by cinching the torso and creating an optical illusion with the flare bottom. Slip on a heel like Holmes to emphasize this characteristic by discreetly adding a few more inches (opt for a jean that’s slightly longer than your usual length). Not only does this denim style make you appear taller, but if you secure the right pair, they’re comfy, too. Unlike restricting skinny jeans, wide-leg styles let your legs breathe, allowing for movement from the thighs down. 

Madewell Super Wide-Leg Jeans

NYDJ Teresa Wide-Leg Jeans

Another key attribute of Holmes’ bottoms? Their dark-wash color. I know what you’re thinking, light jeans are just so cool! While that’s true, inky-blue denim is certainly more elevated, bringing class and sophistication to a laid-back closet staple. Just look at Kate Middleton, who basically only wears dim-shaded jeans. By opting for darker denim, like these wide-leg Madewell pants, your bottoms can be dressed both up and down for further versatility.     

You may be hesitant to integrate such a baggy, dark style into your wardrobe; after all, I once was — all I knew was light, fitted jeans. But I soon came to realize just how easy these flattering bottoms are to style. Tuck a plain white T-shirt into a high-waisted pair for a chic look, reach for slim flats to play with proportions, and you're good to go. You can also dress them up by wearing a sexy mesh top, slipping on a pair of pumps, and adding a chunky belt. You really can’t go wrong with styling, making wide-leg, dark-wash jeans one of the easiest pieces in your closet.
